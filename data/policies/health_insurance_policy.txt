SECURELIFE HEALTH INSURANCE POLICY

Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Expiration Date: December 31, 2025

SECTION 1 – DEFINITIONS

"Policyholder" refers to the individual or entity named in the policy declarations who has purchased this insurance coverage.

"Covered Person" means the policyholder and any eligible dependents listed on the policy, including spouse and children under 26 years of age.

"Pre-existing Condition" means any illness, injury, or medical condition that was diagnosed, treated, or for which symptoms were present within the 24 months prior to the policy effective date.

"Network Provider" means a healthcare provider, hospital, or facility that has a contractual agreement with SecureLife Insurance to provide services at negotiated rates.

"Deductible" means the amount the Covered Person must pay out-of-pocket before the insurance company begins to pay its share of covered expenses. The annual deductible for this policy is $1,500 per individual and $3,000 per family.

"Copayment" means a fixed amount paid by the Covered Person at the time of receiving a covered healthcare service.

"Coinsurance" means the percentage of costs shared between the insurance company and the Covered Person after the deductible has been met. The standard coinsurance rate is 80/20 (insurer pays 80%, Covered Person pays 20%).

SECTION 2 – COVERAGE DETAILS

2.1 Inpatient Hospitalization
Coverage includes room and board, nursing services, operating room charges, anesthesia, medications administered during the stay, laboratory tests, and diagnostic imaging. The policy covers up to 180 days of inpatient hospitalization per policy year. Copayment: $250 per admission for Network Providers; $500 per admission for Out-of-Network Providers.

2.2 Outpatient Services
Coverage includes physician office visits, specialist consultations, diagnostic tests, outpatient surgery, physical therapy (up to 30 sessions per year), occupational therapy (up to 20 sessions per year), and speech therapy (up to 20 sessions per year). Copayments: $30 for primary care visits, $50 for specialist visits at Network Providers.

2.3 Emergency Services
Emergency room visits are covered at all hospitals regardless of network status. Copayment: $150 per visit (waived if admitted). Ambulance services covered up to $2,000 per incident.

2.4 Prescription Drug Coverage
Tier 1 (Generic): $10 copay per 30-day supply
Tier 2 (Preferred Brand): $35 copay per 30-day supply
Tier 3 (Non-Preferred Brand): $60 copay per 30-day supply
Tier 4 (Specialty): 25% coinsurance up to $250 per 30-day supply
Mail-order prescriptions available at 2.5x copay for a 90-day supply.

2.5 Mental Health and Substance Abuse
Coverage includes inpatient treatment (up to 30 days per year), outpatient therapy sessions (up to 52 visits per year), and medication management. Copayments are the same as general outpatient services.

2.6 Preventive Care
Annual physical examinations, immunizations, cancer screenings (mammography, colonoscopy, Pap smear, PSA test), well-child visits, and prenatal care are covered at 100% with no copayment or deductible when using Network Providers.

2.7 Maternity Care
Prenatal visits, delivery (vaginal or cesarean), postnatal care, and newborn care are covered. Hospital stays: up to 48 hours for vaginal delivery, up to 96 hours for cesarean delivery.

2.8 Dental and Vision (Optional Rider)
Basic dental: preventive cleanings (2 per year), X-rays, fillings. Major dental: crowns, bridges, root canals (50% coinsurance). Vision: annual eye exam, $150 allowance for frames/lenses or contact lenses every 24 months.

SECTION 3 – EXCLUSIONS

The following are NOT covered under this policy:

3.1 Cosmetic surgery or procedures performed solely for aesthetic purposes, unless required due to accidental injury or congenital defect.

3.2 Experimental or investigational treatments not approved by the FDA.

3.3 Services received outside the United States, except for emergency care during travel (limited to $50,000 per incident).

3.4 Injuries sustained while participating in extreme sports including but not limited to: base jumping, free solo rock climbing, wing suit flying, and professional motorsports.

3.5 Weight loss surgery (bariatric surgery) unless the Covered Person has a BMI of 40 or greater, or BMI of 35 with documented obesity-related health conditions, and has completed a supervised weight management program of at least 6 months.

3.6 Long-term custodial care or nursing home care beyond 60 days per policy year.

3.7 Work-related injuries or illnesses covered under Workers' Compensation.

3.8 Self-inflicted injuries, except where resulting from a documented mental health condition.

SECTION 4 – CLAIMS PROCEDURE

4.1 Filing a Claim: Claims must be submitted within 90 days of receiving covered services. Claims can be filed online at www.securelife.com/claims, by mail, or by phone at 1-800-555-LIFE.

4.2 Required Documentation: Itemized bill from provider, proof of payment (if applicable), referral or pre-authorization (if required), and completed claim form.

4.3 Processing Time: Claims are typically processed within 30 business days. Complex claims requiring investigation may take up to 60 business days.

4.4 Appeals: If a claim is denied, the Covered Person has the right to appeal within 180 days of the denial notice. First-level appeals are reviewed within 30 days. Second-level appeals (external review) are reviewed by an independent organization within 45 days.

SECTION 5 – PREMIUMS AND PAYMENT

Monthly premium: $450 per individual, $1,100 per family.
Payment due by the 1st of each month. A 31-day grace period applies.
Non-payment after the grace period will result in policy cancellation.
Annual out-of-pocket maximum: $6,500 per individual, $13,000 per family.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SECTION 6 – POLICY RENEWAL AND CANCELLATION

This policy is guaranteed renewable. Premiums may be adjusted at renewal based on age, geographic location, and overall claims experience. The policyholder may cancel at any time with 30 days written notice and receive a pro-rated premium refund. SecureLife may only cancel for non-payment of premiums or material misrepresentation on the application.
